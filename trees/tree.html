Whenever we do not specify exactly what a Tree is, and just refer to it as a "Tree", we mean the Tree Data structure representation of the Tree ADT. Also in practice, most(but not all) Tree ADT are implemented using the Tree Data structure. Hence they essentially mean the same, but it is important to keep the above points mind before we proceed further.

Tree as Abstract Data Type

A Tree Abstract Data Type is a collection of elements, with one of them being designated as the root and hierarchically related to collection sub elements, which themselves are trees. Collection here means any number of elements, be it 0 element(s), 1 element(s) or any number of positive elements.

The collection of sub elements referred above are sub trees, and the roots of those sub trees are referred to as the children of the root. In general, any element in a tree is referred to as a node, and the roots of sub trees hierarchically related to this node are referred to as children of the node. Convenient definition is based on hierarchical relation i.e nodes, one level below in the hierarchy are referred to as children, conversely, the node immediately one level up the hierarchy is called the parent node.

The property of tree prohibits the node from having two parents, forming cycles in the hierarchy and enforces uni-directionality in the hierarchy enforcement.

This is a generic definition of tree, before we proceed to specific trees as lot of literature does not distinguish between a tree and binary tree and co.
